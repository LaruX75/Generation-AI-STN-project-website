Research

The Generation project is part of the Strategic Research Council's Shield programme

The Generation AI project addresses three interconnected societal challenges:

  • The weakening of security, control, and sense of agency in a data-driven society
  • The erosion of trust in authorities, media, science, civil society, and citizens, and
  • Rapidly growing inequality related to the mastery and agency in constantly evolving information and communication technology.  

The project establishes a foundation upon which technology education for the "AI generation" can be built: technology education that considers the functioning principles of artificial intelligence, the opportunities it offers and its dynamic impacts – without forgetting its shortcomings and risks or how AI systems can increase polarization, discrimination, insecurity, and erode trust.

The project brings together technology developers, schools, authorities, businesses, and civil society organizations. It strengthens children's and young people's ability to face the global societal impacts of technology and produces pedagogical tools for teachers in response to the transformation of learning.

Enabling children and young people to understand technology, on which future society is increasingly dependent, is one of the most significant challenges of the 21st century. Inspired by this challenge, the "Generation AI" project produces and sharpens scientific understanding that enables educational solutions responsive to the competence needs of the AI generation.

Read more about the project

Generation AI is part of the Strategic Research Council's Shield programme

Generation AI is a project funded by the Strategic Research Council (STN) operating in connection with the Academy of Finland. Generation AI is one of the projects of the Security and Trust in the Age of Algorithms (Shield) programme.

The Shield programme focuses on security and trust in a world of information technologies and hybrid influence. In addition to more traditional security threats, the threats and opportunities created by digitalization and technological development, as well as various crises from biodiversity loss to climate change, financial crises, and geopolitical conflicts, are reshaping the operational environment in unpredictable ways.

27.05.2025

The Generation AI project promoting AI literacy in children and young people has won the 2025 national Open Educational Resource Award.

The Generation AI project promoting AI literacy in children and young people has won the 2025 national Open Educational Resource Award. The award was presented on Tuesday, May 27, at the Open Science Summer Days in Helsinki. According to the award presenters, the Generation AI project is highly timely, the materials are openly available, and they are based on co-design, which is why the award for open educational material of the year was decided to be given to this project.
