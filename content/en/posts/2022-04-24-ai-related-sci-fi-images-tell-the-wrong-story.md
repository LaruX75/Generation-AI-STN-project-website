<p>Project researcher Prof. Teemu Roos from the University of Helsinki discussed images associated with AI in an FCAI interview in February 2022</p>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p>"Images associated with AI have long been a thorn in my side. More than a decade ago, when I first taught the Introduction to Artificial Intelligence course, I noticed that many students' perceptions of AI were based on stories by Isaac Asimov and other science fiction authors. Some may have even been disappointed when the course didn't discuss what a robot is allowed to do and what it isn't, but rather that it was largely about mathematics," says computer science professor Teemu Roos from the University of Helsinki. Roos also leads <a href="https://fcai.fi/education">AI education at Finland's AI Center FCAI</a>.</p>



<p>&nbsp;The ideas of intelligent robots that began in 1960s science fiction literature, which could threaten humanity, persist stubbornly in discussions about AI. The extent of this can be demonstrated by doing a Google search for the word artificial intelligence.</p>
